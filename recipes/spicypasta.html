Spicy Pasta Perfect for Meal Prep

This spicy spaghetti recipe is one that I make all the time. It's simple, easy, and surprisingly delicious. It's best served fresh, but can store in the freezer up to three months and can be re-heated easily.

Ingredients

  • 28 Oz can San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 lb (around 4 links) good spicy italian sausage
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1/2 tablespoon Red Pepper flakes
  • Good olive oil
  • 2 Tablespoon honey
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • Fresh Basil

Instructions

  1. Heat a high-walled saucepan to medium-high heat. Cover bottom of pan with olive oil. Once the pan is up to temperature, add italian sausage links to the pan. Heat until nearly cooked through and the outside is a rich golden-brown, about 10 minutes
  2. While the sausage is cooking, mince garlic and measure out red pepper flakes. Once sausage is nearly cooked through, add Garlic and red pepper flake and fry in oil for one minute, stirring constantly
  3. After Garlic is browned and oil is fragrent from red pepper flake, add in 28 oz. can of tomatoes followered by the two tablespoons of honey. Stir to ensure garlic is off the bottom of the pan.
  4. Cook until the acidity of the tomatoes have mellowed out and the sausage links are fully cooked through, about 20 minutes. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve over your favorite pasta and add fresh basil as a garnish. Leftover sauce can be saved in the freezer up to three months.
